Implementing a Trading Algorithm

July 31, 2021
The Project:

By late 2020, a close friend of mine asked for help implementing a trading strategy he had thought of. I eagerly accepted the challenge. I can’t say, however, that I expected it to be extremely profitable. His idea was so simple, it was difficult to believe that it would fare well in a market filled with sophisticated trading algorithms. But, well.. it turned out to do just that.

This project is divided in two different parts: the backtesting of the strategy; and the implementation of the trading algorithm.

The Strategy:

The magic formula investing website provides a daily list of stocks ranked in the top 50 according to two formulas: ROI and Earnings Yield. The original idea was to buy some of the stocks in the list and keep them for a year. Our strategy (which we decided to call it Prestige), takes advantage of the momentum that new stocks gain when entering the list. More specifically, we buy new stocks that hadn’t been on the list for at least a week, and sell them one week later.

Backtesting:

A backtest was done for a period of 20 years, on 5488 US stocks, with data extracted from Refinitiv. This amounts to over 28 million observations.

Implementation:

To implement the strategy, a daily code is automatically run on the cloud using IBM Watson Studio. The top 50 stocks are scraped from the magic formula website, and saved in a database. Then, the algorithm is put to work and provides us, via email, which stocks to buy and to sell.
